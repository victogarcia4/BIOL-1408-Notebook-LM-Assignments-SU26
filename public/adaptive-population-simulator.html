The Peppered Moth & Industrial Melanism — A Story of Natural Selection in Real Time.

The Peppered Moth Story

Before the Industrial Revolution, England's birch trees had pale, lichen-covered bark. Light-colored peppered moths (Biston betularia) were nearly invisible, while dark moths were easy targets for birds. Then, factory soot blackened the trees.

Suddenly, dark moths were camouflaged and light moths stood out. Within decades, the population shifted dramatically — dark moths went from rare to dominant. This is natural selection in action: differential survival leading to a change in allele frequencies.

Learning Objective

Evaluate how selective pressures operating on a population lead to differential reproductive success and a shift in allele frequencies over time.
